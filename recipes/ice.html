Ice Recipe

Description

Sometimes you just need to make some ice.
Here's some instructions on how to do that.

Ingredients

  • Water

Steps

  1. Clean the ice tray:Ensure your ice cube tray is clean. Any residue in the tray could potentially end up in your ice cubes, affecting their taste and clarity.
  2. Fill the ice tray:Fill each compartment of the ice cube tray with cold water. Don't overfill because water expands as it freezes.
  3. Freeze the water:Carefully place the ice tray into your freezer. Try to keep it on a flat surface so the water doesn't spill out.
  4. Wait:Leave the water to freeze. This will usually take about 2-4 hours, depending on your freezer settings and the size of the ice cubes.
  5. Remove the ice cubes:Once the water is fully frozen, you can remove the ice tray from the freezer. Twist the tray or push the bottom of each compartment to dislodge the ice cubes.

Congratulations, you've made ice! Remember that for best taste, it's a good idea to use filtered water, especially if your tap water has a strong taste or odor. And, if you need clear ice cubes for cocktail purposes, you might need to use a method called directional freezing, which is a little more complex.
